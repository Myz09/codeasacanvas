What if code wasn't just about solving problems, but expressing who you are?

Code as a Canvas is a celebration of how coding can serve as a form of self-expression, where logic meets imagination and functionality gives way to emotion. Whether you're designing a beautiful UI, building a rich interactive experience, or creating a tool that reflects your identity, this hackathon is your blank canvas.

Throughout the week, we'll have workshops, fun challenges, and a community of creatives to support you!

submission guidelines

  • projects must be made during the hackathon & submitted by 11:59pm EST on march 21, 2026 on devpost
  • participants can submit up to 2 projects. projects can be submitted for multiple bonus tracks.
  • teams of up to 4 people must be registered and signed up on devpost
  • submissions must include a github repo (or figma/canva/project link), demo video, and reflection responses
  • judging criteria revealed at opening ceremony
  • if using AI, please disclose; only minimal usage is allowed

tracks

design

the no-code track, ideal for beginners! design beautiful UI/UX mockups using figma, canva, or your tool of choice.

code

build something beautiful and/or usable, whether it's a website, app, or digital tool. focus on experience (and maybe functionality!)

frequently asked questions

what if i don't have any coding experience?

that's totally fine! we have a design track perfect for beginners, and mentors + resources are available if you want to try the code track.

who is eligible to compete?

this hackathon is open to middle & high school girls or gender expansive individuals.

do i need a team?

nope! you can go solo or form a team of up to 4. join our discord to find teammates!

is there a cost to enter?

nope! isn't it amazing to have access to this opportunity :)

what's the point of this hackathon?

we hope you can walk away with a unique hackathon experience, with projects to display on your portfolios, and most importantly... redefine what coding means!

can i come with an idea?

yes, but ensure it adheres to the track(s) you're submitting to & that it is only build during the hackathon (can't work on a pre-existing proj)
